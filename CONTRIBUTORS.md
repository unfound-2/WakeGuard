# Contributors

and Codex) under their direction, plus physical-build and branding help from two more people. This file
summarizes who contributed what. It is grounded in the git history so the record stays verifiable and
consistent regardless of how any prose log is later edited.

## Aaron Hua — originator and primary author

Aaron originated the product and authored the majority of the codebase and its entire hardware and firmware
half. Git first-authorship confirms the core-engineering files are his: the Arduino firmware
(`WakeGuardClock.ino`), the BLE protocol (`ble_payloads.dart`), and the sync engine (`clock_sync.dart`).

- The physical clock build and wiring.
- The autonomous Arduino/HM-10 firmware (software timekeeping with drift calibration, EEPROM persistence,
  on-device dismissal-token security).
- The hand-rolled framed BLE protocol, proven byte-for-byte in Dart and C++.
- The sync/connectivity engine, on-demand BLE, timers, backup notifications, and the real dismissal path.
- The alarm system, the wake-challenge design, and the single global backup-code security design.
- The initial app outline, three-way routing, and the Liquid Glass design foundation.
- The production audit/hardening, the test suite, and the original project documentation.

Full detail: `AARON_UPDATE_LOG.md`.

## A note on AI authorship

Much of the code was generated by AI assistants rather than hand-written by either primary contributor.
Under current US guidance, purely AI-generated code is not itself copyrightable; the human contribution is
the design, architecture, selection/arrangement, hardware, and product decisions. Credit here reflects that
human direction and the non-AI work (notably Aaron's hardware and firmware).
